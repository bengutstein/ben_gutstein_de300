
The Chicago Cubs are a professional baseball team based in Chicago, Illinois.
They compete in Major League Baseball (MLB) as a member club of the National League (NL) Central division.
The team was founded in 1876 and has played its home games at Wrigley Field since 1916.
The Cubs are known for their loyal fan base and long history, including their infamous 108-year World Series championship drought,
which ended in 2016 when they defeated the Cleveland Indians in a thrilling seven-game series.
Wrigley Field, with its iconic ivy-covered outfield walls and hand-turned scoreboard, is one of the most historic ballparks in America.
